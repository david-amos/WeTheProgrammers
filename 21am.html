21st Amendment

The eighteenth article of amendment to the Constitution of the United States is hereby repealed.

The transportation or importation into any State, Territory, or possession of the United States for delivery or use therein of intoxicating liquors, in violation of the laws thereof, is hereby prohibited.

This article shall be inoperative unless it shall have been ratified as an amendment to the Constitution by conventions in the several States, as provided in the Constitution, within seven years from the date of the submission hereof to the States by the Congress.

Why do we have this amendment

The 21st Amendment was passed to end national Prohibition. In the beginning national sobriety sounded great and was a good thing but after a few years illegal distribution and consumption by both the rich and the poor became overwhelming. The american people wanted their alcohol and they were not going to let the government keep it from them any longer so we as a country decided we should end Prohibition.
